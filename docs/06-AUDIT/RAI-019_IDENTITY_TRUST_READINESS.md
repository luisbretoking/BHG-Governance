# 1. Audit Objective

This audit evaluates identity and trust readiness within the repository
according to the Repository Audit Intelligence framework.

The objective is to determine whether the engineering system provides the
foundations required to support digital identity and trust capabilities
through:

- Identity management principles.
- Evidence-based verification.
- Trust models.
- Secure records.
- User reputation concepts.


---

# 2. Audit Scope

The evaluation covers:

- Identity architecture principles.
- Trust model foundations.
- Data integrity requirements.
- Security considerations.
- Verification concepts.


This audit does not evaluate:

- Production identity providers.
- Legal identity validation.
- Biometric systems.
- Regulatory identity certification.


---

# 3. Audit Methodology

The assessment follows the RAI identity evaluation process:

1. Review identity architecture principles.
2. Evaluate trust requirements.
3. Analyze verification foundations.
4. Identify identity risks.
5. Document recommendations.

# 5. Findings


## RAI-019-F001

**Category:** Digital Identity Foundation  
**Severity:** High  
**Status:** Resolved


### Finding

The ecosystem vision requires identity and trust capabilities to be explicitly
evaluated as a separate engineering dimension.


### Impact

Without identity governance, future systems may experience:

- Weak user verification.
- Unreliable reputation models.
- Poor data ownership.


### Resolution

Established Identity & Trust Readiness as an official RAI assessment
dimension.

The evaluation considers:

- Identity architecture.
- Verification evidence.
- Trust relationships.
- Secure records.


### Result

Identity and trust are recognized as strategic engineering capabilities.


---

## RAI-019-F002

**Category:** Trust Intelligence  
**Severity:** Medium  
**Status:** Monitoring


### Finding

Advanced trust systems require additional models and operational processes.


### Impact

Future trust platforms may require:

- Evidence scoring.
- Reputation lifecycle management.
- Fraud prevention mechanisms.


### Recommendation

Future improvements:

- Identity lifecycle framework.
- Trust graph models.
- Verification workflows.
- Evidence management system.
- Privacy-preserving identity mechanisms.


### Result

Identity foundation exists with future specialization requirements.
