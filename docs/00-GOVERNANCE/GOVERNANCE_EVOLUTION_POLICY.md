# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the official framework governing how Breto's Holding Group evolves its governance system.

Its objective is to ensure that every governance modification remains transparent, auditable, traceable, evidence-based and aligned with the long-term vision of the Holding.

Governance shall evolve continuously without compromising institutional stability.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to every governance artifact maintained by Breto's Holding Group, including but not limited to:

- Constitutions
- Governance documents
- Policies
- Standards
- Procedures
- Engineering rules
- AI governance
- Corporate documentation

Every governance change shall comply with this policy.

---

# 3. Governance Principles

Governance exists to enable sustainable innovation.

Governance shall facilitate execution rather than create unnecessary bureaucracy.

Stable principles shall support evolvable rules.

Every governance decision shall be supported by evidence.

Every governance change shall improve institutional consistency.

Every governance change shall remain permanently auditable.

---

# 4. Stable Principles, Evolvable Rules

Breto's Holding Group distinguishes between permanent institutional principles and operational rules.

Institutional principles shall remain highly stable.

Policies, standards and procedures may evolve whenever justified by sufficient evidence.

The governance framework shall encourage continuous improvement without sacrificing consistency.

---

# 5. Evolution Triggers

Governance evolution may be initiated when one or more of the following conditions exist:

- Regulatory changes
- Security improvements
- Operational inefficiencies
- Engineering discoveries
- AI recommendations supported by evidence
- Company expansion
- Organizational restructuring
- Risk mitigation
- Innovation opportunities
- Lessons learned

---

# 6. Proposal Requirements

Every governance proposal shall clearly define:

- Problem statement
- Motivation
- Expected benefits
- Risks
- Impacted documents
- Impacted companies
- Implementation strategy
- Migration requirements
- Supporting evidence

Incomplete proposals shall not proceed to review.

---

# 7. Governance Proposal Lifecycle

Every governance modification shall follow the official lifecycle:

1. Problem Identification
2. Proposal Creation
3. Evidence Collection
4. Discussion
5. Technical Review
6. Governance Review
7. Approval
8. Merge
9. Version Update
10. Changelog Update
11. Publication
12. Effective Date

Skipping lifecycle stages is prohibited unless Emergency Change procedures apply.

---

# 8. Evidence First

Opinions alone shall never justify governance changes.

Evidence may include:

- Technical analysis
- Audits
- Security reports
- Performance metrics
- Regulatory requirements
- Engineering experience
- Operational lessons
- AI recommendations validated by humans

Human authority always prevails over AI recommendations.

---

# 9. Traceability

Every governance modification shall preserve complete historical traceability.

Each approved modification shall reference:

- Proposal ID
- Document Version
- Previous Version
- Commit
- Pull Request
- Author
- Reviewer
- Approver
- Approval Date
- Effective Date

Historical information shall never be deleted.

---

# 10. Version Management

All governance documents shall follow Semantic Versioning.

Major versions indicate breaking governance changes.

Minor versions introduce compatible improvements.

Patch versions correct documentation without changing governance behavior.

---

# 11. Continuous Improvement

Every team is encouraged to identify opportunities for improving governance.

Whenever an existing governance rule unnecessarily limits innovation, scalability, maintainability or security, teams shall formally submit an improvement proposal.

Continuous improvement is considered an institutional responsibility.

---

# 12. Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory across the entire Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Non-compliant governance changes shall not become official documentation.

---

# 13. Exceptions

Exceptions require explicit approval from the Governance Council.

Emergency exceptions shall be documented and reviewed after implementation.

Undocumented exceptions are prohibited.
