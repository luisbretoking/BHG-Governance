# SubHolding Model

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the organizational model for every SubHolding operating within Breto's Holding Group (BHG).

A SubHolding exists to govern one strategic business domain while remaining fully aligned with the constitutional principles of the Holding.

Each SubHolding operates independently within its own domain but never independently from the governance architecture established by BHG.

---

# 2. Definition

A SubHolding is an autonomous organizational entity responsible for developing, governing and operating one strategic ecosystem inside BHG.

Examples include:

- Financial Infrastructure
- Media & Entertainment
- Artificial Intelligence
- Healthcare
- Education
- Logistics

Each ecosystem may contain multiple companies, products, services, technologies and business units.

---

# 3. Mission

The mission of every SubHolding is to maximize long-term value creation within its strategic domain while preserving alignment with the Holding Constitution.

---

# 4. Governance Principles

Every SubHolding shall:

- Respect the BHG Constitution.
- Respect the Legal Hierarchy.
- Respect the Authority Model.
- Respect mandatory Foundation documents.
- Preserve ecosystem interoperability.
- Protect corporate reputation.
- Contribute to the long-term mission of BHG.

No SubHolding may create governance rules that contradict Foundation-level governance.

---

# 5. Autonomy

Each SubHolding possesses operational autonomy.

Operational autonomy includes:

- Organizational design
- Technical architecture
- Internal standards
- Product portfolio
- Internal governance
- AI governance
- Engineering standards
- Brand identity
- Commercial strategy

provided that none of these contradict Foundation documents.

---

# 6. Constitutional Authority

Every SubHolding may publish its own Constitution.

The SubHolding Constitution:

- extends Foundation governance;
- never replaces Foundation governance;
- may define stricter rules;
- may define additional governance layers;
- may never reduce mandatory Foundation requirements.

---

# 7. Organizational Structure

A SubHolding may contain:

- Companies
- Subsidiaries
- Business Units
- Platforms
- Products
- Services
- AI Systems
- Internal Committees
- Engineering Organizations

The internal structure may evolve without requiring changes to the Foundation architecture.

---

# 8. Governance Council

Every SubHolding shall establish its own Governance Council.

Responsibilities include:

- Local governance
- Architecture decisions
- Standards approval
- Policy approval
- Risk management
- Strategic planning

The Council acts independently within its delegated authority.

Strategic decisions affecting BHG require escalation to the BHG Governance Council.

---

# 9. Relationship with BHG

The relationship follows these principles:

BHG defines principles.

SubHoldings define implementation.

BHG defines governance.

SubHoldings define execution.

BHG defines authority.

SubHoldings define operations.

---

# 10. AI Governance

Every SubHolding may operate independent AI systems.

Those systems shall:

- comply with the AI Constitution;
- comply with Holding governance;
- remain auditable;
- preserve human authority;
- maintain decision traceability.

No AI system may replace authorized human governance.

---

# 11. Financial Responsibility

Every SubHolding shall be financially accountable for:

- budgets;
- investments;
- risks;
- assets;
- liabilities;
- operational sustainability.

Financial reporting shall remain compatible with Holding reporting standards.

---

# 12. Evolution

SubHoldings are expected to evolve continuously.

Structural evolution shall preserve:

- governance compatibility;
- legal compatibility;
- documentation compatibility;
- interoperability.

Evolution shall never compromise ecosystem integrity.

---

# 13. Compliance

Compliance is mandatory.

Violations shall follow the Governance Escalation Model and Governance Conflict Resolution procedures.

---

# 14. Guiding Principle

Strong autonomy.

One Constitution.

One ecosystem.

One long-term vision.
