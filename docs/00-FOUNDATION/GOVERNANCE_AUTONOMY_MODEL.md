# Governance Autonomy Model

> Defines the official autonomy boundaries for every governed entity within the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance autonomy model adopted by Breto's Holding Group.

Its purpose is to establish which governance responsibilities remain exclusively under BHG authority and which may be exercised independently by governed entities.

The model promotes innovation without compromising institutional consistency.

---

# Core Principle

Governance authority is federated.

Normative supremacy remains centralized.

Operational autonomy is decentralized.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Every governed entity possesses the right to develop its own governance framework.

That autonomy exists only within the constitutional boundaries established by Breto's Holding Group.

Autonomy enables adaptation.

The Constitution preserves institutional identity.

---

# Reserved Authorities

The following authorities remain exclusively under BHG Governance:

- Constitutional principles
- Governance hierarchy
- Normative authority model
- Corporate identity
- Intellectual property governance
- Governance interoperability
- Enterprise-wide standards
- Ecosystem policies

These authorities cannot be overridden.

---

# Delegated Authorities

Governed entities may independently establish:

- Internal constitutions
- Organizational structures
- Governance councils
- Internal regulations
- Operational procedures
- Engineering practices
- Technical standards
- Product governance
- Project governance
- Service governance

Provided that none contradict higher-level governance.

---

# Constitutional Compliance

Every governance artifact created by a governed entity shall comply with:

- BHG Constitution
- Governance Model
- Authority Model
- Legal Hierarchy
- Federated Governance Model

Compliance is mandatory.

---

# Conflict Resolution

When governance conflicts occur:

1. Constitutional authority prevails.
2. Higher governance level prevails.
3. Explicit authority prevails.
4. New governance shall never invalidate superior governance.

Conflicts shall be resolved according to the Governance Conflict Resolution process.

---

# Innovation Principle

Governed entities are encouraged to innovate.

Innovation shall never require modifying constitutional governance unless constitutional evolution is formally approved.

---

# Governance Independence

Entities may define:

- Internal committees
- Internal approval workflows
- Internal operational policies
- Engineering methodologies
- AI operating procedures
- Product lifecycle models

These decisions remain local unless they affect ecosystem-wide governance.

---

# Governance Responsibilities

Every governed entity shall:

- Maintain governance documentation.
- Preserve traceability.
- Identify governance authority.
- Maintain version history.
- Preserve documentary integrity.
- Support governance audits.

---

# Governance Evolution

Local governance may evolve independently.

Corporate governance evolves only through the official governance process.

Autonomy shall never become constitutional fragmentation.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Governance is centralized in authority and decentralized in execution.

> Sustainable ecosystems empower local innovation while preserving institutional unity.
