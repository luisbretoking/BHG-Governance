# Subsidiary Model

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official organizational model for Subsidiaries operating inside a SubHolding within Breto's Holding Group (BHG).

A Subsidiary exists to execute specific business activities while remaining fully aligned with the governance framework of its parent SubHolding and the Holding Constitution.

---

# 2. Definition

A Subsidiary is an operational legal or organizational entity responsible for delivering products, services or platforms within a defined business scope.

A Subsidiary may own:

- Products
- Services
- Platforms
- Internal systems
- Teams
- Operational assets
- Intellectual property assigned by its parent organization

---

# 3. Purpose of a Subsidiary

Every Subsidiary exists to transform strategic objectives into operational execution.

Its responsibilities include:

- Building products
- Delivering services
- Managing customers
- Operating technology
- Executing commercial activities
- Generating sustainable value

---

# 4. Governance Relationship

Each Subsidiary reports directly to its parent SubHolding.

Governance authority follows this hierarchy:

BHG
→ SubHolding
→ Subsidiary

Subsidiaries shall never bypass governance levels.

---

# 5. Autonomy

Subsidiaries possess operational autonomy regarding:

- Internal organization
- Daily operations
- Product development
- Customer management
- Resource allocation
- Technical implementation

provided these remain compatible with higher governance levels.

---

# 6. Constitutional Compliance

Every Subsidiary shall comply with:

- Foundation documents
- Holding Policies
- Engineering Standards
- AI Governance
- SubHolding Constitution

Additional internal rules may be created but shall never reduce mandatory requirements.

---

# 7. Organizational Structure

Each Subsidiary may contain:

- Departments
- Teams
- Projects
- Products
- Platforms
- Operational Units

Its structure shall remain documented and auditable.

---

# 8. Governance Responsibilities

Each Subsidiary shall:

- maintain documentation;
- manage operational risks;
- report performance;
- protect corporate assets;
- maintain traceability;
- preserve governance integrity.

---

# 9. Decision Authority

Subsidiaries may independently decide:

- operational improvements;
- implementation details;
- internal processes;
- project execution.

Strategic decisions affecting the SubHolding or BHG require escalation.

---

# 10. AI Systems

Subsidiaries may deploy AI systems.

Those systems shall:

- remain auditable;
- preserve human authority;
- follow AI governance policies;
- document recommendations;
- never become governance authorities.

AI supports decisions.

Humans make decisions.

---

# 11. Financial Responsibility

Each Subsidiary shall be accountable for:

- operational budgets;
- financial performance;
- assets;
- liabilities;
- compliance.

Financial reporting shall follow Holding standards.

---

# 12. Evolution

Subsidiaries are expected to evolve continuously.

Structural evolution shall preserve:

- interoperability;
- documentation quality;
- governance compatibility;
- traceability.

---

# 13. Performance Measurement

Every Subsidiary shall define measurable objectives including:

- Operational KPIs
- Financial KPIs
- Product KPIs
- Governance KPIs
- Risk Indicators

Performance measurement shall support continuous improvement.

---

# 14. Compliance

Non-compliance shall follow:

- Governance Escalation Model
- Governance Conflict Resolution
- Change Management Policy

---

# 15. Guiding Principle

Execute independently.

Govern responsibly.

Scale sustainably.

Remain aligned.
