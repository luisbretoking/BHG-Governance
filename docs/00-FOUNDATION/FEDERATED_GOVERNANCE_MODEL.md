# Federated Governance Model

> Defines how normative authority is distributed throughout the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the federated governance model adopted by Breto's Holding Group.

Its purpose is to establish how governance authority originates, how it flows across the ecosystem and how autonomous entities may create their own governance frameworks while preserving institutional consistency.

The model enables decentralized innovation without compromising corporate governance.

---

# Vision

Create a governance ecosystem where every organizational entity can evolve independently while remaining aligned with the constitutional principles established by Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Core Principles

## Constitutional Supremacy

The BHG Constitution is the highest normative authority of the ecosystem.

No subordinate governance artifact may contradict its principles.

---

## Federated Autonomy

Every authorized entity may establish its own governance framework.

Autonomy includes the creation of:

- Constitutions
- Governance Models
- Policies
- Standards
- Procedures
- Protocols
- Operational Rules

Provided that such governance remains compatible with the corporate governance framework.

---

## Principle of Non-Contradiction

Lower governance levels may extend, refine or specialize corporate governance.

They shall never invalidate, weaken or contradict higher-level governance artifacts.

---

## Delegated Authority

Governance authority originates exclusively from Breto's Holding Group.

Specific governance responsibilities may be delegated to organizational entities according to the Authority Model.

Delegation never transfers constitutional ownership.

---

## Institutional Consistency

Every governance artifact shall preserve:

- Traceability
- Accountability
- Version history
- Normative hierarchy
- Documentary integrity

---

# Governance Domains

The ecosystem is organized into governance domains.

Examples include:

- Breto's Holding Group
- Subsidiaries
- Subholdings
- Business Units
- Platforms
- Services
- Projects
- AI Systems

Each domain may establish local governance according to its assigned authority.

---

# Governance Hierarchy

Normative authority flows downward through the following hierarchy:

1. BHG Constitution
2. Corporate Governance Models
3. Corporate Policies
4. Corporate Standards
5. Domain Constitutions
6. Domain Governance Models
7. Domain Policies
8. Domain Standards
9. Operational Procedures
10. Implementations

Compliance flows upward.

---

# Governance Boundaries

The following responsibilities remain exclusive to Breto's Holding Group:

- Constitutional governance
- Corporate identity
- Governance architecture
- Authority allocation
- Corporate legal hierarchy
- Enterprise governance principles

Organizational entities may govern all remaining operational matters within these boundaries.

---

# Domain Governance

Each governance domain shall define:

- Governance owner
- Approval authority
- Scope
- Responsibilities
- Decision process
- Relationship with higher authorities

---

# Conflict Resolution

When governance conflicts arise:

1. The highest applicable normative authority prevails.
2. Constitutional principles override all subordinate governance.
3. Domain governance shall be updated to restore compliance.
4. Governance decisions shall remain fully documented.

---

# Governance Evolution

Governance domains may evolve independently.

Every governance change shall:

- preserve traceability;
- identify governing authority;
- document rationale;
- record approvals;
- maintain compatibility with higher governance levels.

---

# AI Participation

Artificial Intelligence systems may:

- analyze governance;
- identify conflicts;
- recommend improvements;
- validate compliance;
- assist documentation.

Artificial Intelligence systems shall never approve governance artifacts.

---

# Repository Integration

Every governance repository within the ecosystem shall:

- identify its governance domain;
- identify its normative parent;
- identify its constitutional root;
- declare governance relationships;
- maintain documentary traceability.

---

# Future Expansion

The federated governance model supports future organizational growth including:

- New companies
- New subsidiaries
- New platforms
- New AI ecosystems
- New governance repositories
- International operations

Without requiring changes to the constitutional foundation.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Sustainable organizations balance centralized authority with decentralized innovation.

> Governance remains unified even when implementation becomes distributed.
