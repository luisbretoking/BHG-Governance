# Legal Hierarchy

> Official Normative Hierarchy Model of Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official normative hierarchy of the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Its purpose is to guarantee that every governance artifact derives its authority from a superior source, creating a coherent, auditable and scalable institutional framework.

Every policy, standard, procedure, guideline and implementation shall be traceable to its original authority source through the established governance hierarchy.

---

# Scope

This hierarchy applies to all governance artifacts created within:

- Breto's Holding Group;
- Subholdings;
- Subsidiaries;
- Corporate repositories;
- Governance systems;
- Artificial Intelligence governance frameworks.

No internal governance artifact may establish authority outside this hierarchy.

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1 — Normative Unity

BHG maintains a single official chain of normative authority.

Parallel or independent normative hierarchies are prohibited.

---

## Principle 2 — Authority Derivation

Every governance artifact shall explicitly identify:

- the documents governing it;
- the documents it governs;
- the authority source from which it derives legitimacy.

---

## Principle 3 — Non-Contradiction

A lower-level governance artifact shall never modify, restrict or contradict a higher-level authority.

---

## Principle 4 — Specialization

Each lower level exists to develop higher-level principles with greater operational detail.

Lower levels extend authority.

They do not replace authority.

---

## Principle 5 — Traceability

Every governance artifact shall allow reconstruction of its complete authority lineage.

# Authority Levels

## Level 1 — Constitution

The Constitution defines the permanent principles of Breto's Holding Group.

It represents the highest internal normative authority.

No governance artifact may contradict constitutional principles.

---

## Level 2 — Foundational Governance Models

Foundational Governance Models develop constitutional principles.

Examples:

- Governance Model;
- Authority Model;
- Legal Hierarchy.

They define how the institutional system operates.

---

## Level 3 — Policies

Policies define what must be done.

They establish mandatory organizational requirements.

---

## Level 4 — Standards

Standards define how requirements shall be fulfilled.

They establish measurable and verifiable criteria.

---

## Level 5 — Procedures

Procedures define the sequence of activities required to execute policies and standards.

---

## Level 6 — Guidelines

Guidelines provide recommendations, examples and best practices.

Their application may be flexible unless explicitly required.

---

## Level 7 — Implementations

Implementations materialize governance requirements through:

- software;
- processes;
- configurations;
- operational systems;
- technological tools.

Implementations cannot redefine governance authority.

---

## Level 8 — Records and Evidence

Records and evidence demonstrate application, compliance and historical execution.

They preserve institutional memory.

They do not create normative authority.

---

# Normative Conflict Resolution

When two governance artifacts conflict, interpretation shall follow these rules:

1. The higher hierarchical level prevails.

2. If artifacts belong to the same level, the more specific artifact prevails.

3. If both have equivalent scope and authority, the most recently approved artifact prevails.

4. If conflict remains unresolved, the competent governance authority defined by the Authority Model shall issue the final determination.

---

# Normative Dependencies

Every official governance document shall contain an explicit authority section.

The section shall identify:

## Governed By

Documents with superior authority.

## Governs

Documents that derive authority from the current document.

---

# Governance Evolution

Normative modifications shall follow a top-down evolution model.

Higher-level changes shall be evaluated before lower-level modifications.

When a superior authority document changes, all dependent documents shall undergo compatibility review.

No lower-level artifact may independently redefine higher-level authority.

---

# Artificial Intelligence Compatibility

Artificial Intelligence systems operating within the BHG ecosystem shall use this hierarchy to:

- identify applicable authority;
- resolve document conflicts;
- detect contradictions;
- recommend updates;
- explain governance reasoning.

AI systems shall interpret authority according to this hierarchy but shall not modify normative authority.

---

# Compliance

Any governance artifact violating this hierarchy shall be considered a governance non-conformity.

Such cases shall follow the official Governance Conflict Resolution process.

---

# Final Principle

Normative authority flows from principles toward implementation.

The institutional consistency of Breto's Holding Group depends on preserving this chain of authority without interruption.
