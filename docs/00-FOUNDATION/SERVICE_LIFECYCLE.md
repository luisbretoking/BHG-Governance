# Service Lifecycle

# Purpose

This document defines the official lifecycle for every service within the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

The lifecycle ensures that services are created, operated, improved and retired through controlled governance rather than informal decisions.

Every service shall follow this lifecycle.

---

# Lifecycle Principles

Every service shall be:

- intentional
- documented
- governed
- measurable
- traceable
- secure
- continuously improved

No service may bypass this lifecycle.

---

# Lifecycle Stages

## Stage 1 — Proposal

The need for a new service is identified.

Required outputs:

- business justification
- expected value
- responsible enterprise
- proposed owner
- initial scope

Approval is required before continuing.

---

## Stage 2 — Design

The service architecture is defined.

This includes:

- interfaces
- dependencies
- consumers
- governance
- security
- documentation

No implementation shall begin before design approval.

---

## Stage 3 — Implementation

The service is developed according to engineering standards.

Implementation shall include:

- documentation
- testing
- security validation
- version control

Every implementation shall remain traceable.

---

## Stage 4 — Validation

The service is evaluated before becoming operational.

Validation includes:

- functional verification
- security review
- documentation review
- governance compliance

Human approval remains mandatory.

---

## Stage 5 — Production

The service becomes operational.

During this stage the service shall be monitored continuously.

Metrics shall include:

- availability
- performance
- incidents
- adoption
- customer value

---

## Stage 6 — Continuous Improvement

Operational feedback is continuously collected.

Possible improvements include:

- optimization
- security updates
- usability
- scalability
- cost reduction

Improvements shall follow official governance processes.

---

## Stage 7 — Deprecation

The service is scheduled for retirement.

The following shall be documented:

- replacement
- migration plan
- communication plan
- risks

Consumers shall receive advance notice.

---

## Stage 8 — Retirement

The service is officially discontinued.

Before retirement:

- consumers shall be migrated
- documentation archived
- dependencies resolved
- governance records completed

Historical traceability shall remain permanently available.

---

# Governance Responsibilities

Service Owners manage daily operations.

Enterprises manage implementation.

The Governance Council approves strategic decisions.

BEiA may provide:

- impact analysis
- dependency analysis
- performance insights
- governance recommendations

BEiA shall never approve lifecycle transitions.

Human authority remains final.

---

# Versioning

Every lifecycle change shall follow official versioning policies.

Breaking operational changes require documented approval.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Every service shall maintain:

- purpose
- owner
- lifecycle stage
- architecture
- interfaces
- dependencies
- KPIs
- version history
- change history

Documentation shall remain current throughout the lifecycle.

---

# Success Criteria

A mature service demonstrates:

- operational stability
- measurable value
- documented governance
- security compliance
- continuous improvement
- complete traceability

---

# Compliance

No enterprise within the BHG ecosystem may operate a service outside this lifecycle.

Exceptions require formal approval by the Governance Council.
