# Governance Change Workflow

> Defines the official lifecycle for proposing, reviewing, approving, implementing and auditing governance changes across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

This workflow establishes the mandatory governance lifecycle for every normative modification within the BHG ecosystem.

Its objectives are to:

- preserve governance integrity;
- maintain constitutional compliance;
- ensure complete traceability;
- enable distributed governance;
- support continuous institutional evolution.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every governance change shall be:

- documented;
- reviewable;
- traceable;
- auditable;
- reproducible;
- constitutionally compliant.

No governance modification may bypass this workflow.

---

# Governance Change Lifecycle

Every governance change shall progress through the following stages.

## Stage 1 — Proposal

A governance proposal may originate from:

- BHG Governance Council;
- Company Governance Council;
- Engineering teams;
- Audit findings;
- Artificial Intelligence analysis;
- Operational experience.

Outputs:

- Proposal identifier.
- Problem statement.
- Expected outcome.

---

## Stage 2 — Initial Validation

The proposal shall be evaluated to determine:

- governance scope;
- authority level;
- affected repositories;
- impacted documents;
- implementation complexity.

AI systems may perform preliminary analysis.

---

## Stage 3 — Impact Analysis

A formal impact assessment shall identify:

- affected governance artifacts;
- dependency changes;
- compatibility risks;
- organizational impact;
- migration requirements.

No approval may proceed without documented impact analysis.

---

## Stage 4 — Technical and Governance Review

The proposal shall undergo review by the appropriate governance authority.

Review activities include:

- constitutional compliance;
- policy consistency;
- architectural alignment;
- terminology validation;
- document integrity.

AI systems may provide recommendations but shall not approve changes.

---

## Stage 5 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority shall either:

- approve;
- reject;
- request revision;
- escalate.

Every decision shall be recorded.

---

## Stage 6 — Implementation

Approved changes shall be implemented through controlled modifications.

Implementation activities include:

- document updates;
- metadata updates;
- dependency updates;
- version increment;
- changelog registration.

---

## Stage 7 — Verification

Verification confirms:

- document validity;
- dependency integrity;
- governance consistency;
- repository compliance.

Automated validation should be performed whenever possible.

---

## Stage 8 — Audit

The completed change shall become subject to governance audit.

Audit verifies:

- workflow compliance;
- approval evidence;
- traceability;
- documentation completeness.

---

## Stage 9 — Institutional Adoption

Once verified, the governance change becomes part of the official institutional knowledge of Breto's Holding Group.

Future governance artifacts shall reference the updated version.

---

# Emergency Changes

Emergency governance changes may follow an accelerated workflow.

Accelerated approval shall:

- remain documented;
- preserve traceability;
- undergo post-implementation audit.

Emergency procedures shall never bypass constitutional authority.

---

# Federated Governance Participation

Company Governance Councils may execute this workflow for governance within their delegated authority.

Cross-company changes shall be escalated to the BHG Governance Council.

---

# AI Analytical Participation

Artificial Intelligence systems may:

- detect governance conflicts;
- simulate change impact;
- identify affected documents;
- recommend reviewers;
- classify proposal priority;
- generate compliance reports.

AI systems shall never authorize governance changes.

---

# Required Evidence

Every governance change shall retain:

- proposal identifier;
- affected documents;
- impact assessment;
- approval record;
- implementation evidence;
- audit reference;
- version history.

---

# Governance Integrity

No governance artifact becomes authoritative until this workflow has been successfully completed.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Sustainable governance depends on disciplined evolution.

> Every approved change becomes institutional knowledge through documented process.
