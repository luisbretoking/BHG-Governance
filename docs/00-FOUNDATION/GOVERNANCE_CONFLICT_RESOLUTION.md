# Governance Conflict Resolution

> Official conflict resolution framework of Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official process for identifying, analyzing, escalating and resolving governance conflicts throughout the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Governance conflicts are treated as opportunities to improve institutional clarity, strengthen governance maturity and preserve organizational consistency.

The purpose of conflict resolution is not merely to eliminate disagreement.

Its purpose is to ensure that every disagreement is resolved through documented authority, evidence and institutional principles.

---

# Scope

This process applies to:

- Governance documents;
- Authority disputes;
- Policy conflicts;
- Constitutional interpretation;
- Cross-company governance conflicts;
- Engineering governance conflicts;
- Artificial Intelligence governance conflicts;
- Security governance conflicts;
- Repository governance conflicts.

Every governance conflict affecting the BHG ecosystem shall follow this framework unless a higher authority explicitly defines another process.

---

# Core Principles

Governance conflict resolution shall always be:

## Constitutional

Every resolution shall comply with the BHG Constitution.

---

## Evidence-Based

Decisions shall prioritize documented evidence over personal interpretation.

---

## Human Governed

Final authority belongs exclusively to authorized human governance bodies.

---

## Transparent

Resolution reasoning shall be documented.

---

## Traceable

Every conflict shall maintain complete historical records.

---

## Proportional

Resolution effort shall correspond to the impact and severity of the conflict.

---

# Conflict Types

Governance conflicts may include:

## Authority Conflicts

Disagreements regarding decision rights, responsibilities or approval authority.

---

## Normative Conflicts

Contradictions between governance documents.

Examples:

- Constitution versus Policy conflict;
- Policy versus Standard conflict;
- Standard versus Procedure conflict.

---

## Interpretation Conflicts

Situations where multiple interpretations of a governance principle exist.

---

## Jurisdiction Conflicts

Conflicts regarding responsibility between:

- Companies;
- Subholdings;
- Governance bodies;
- Functional areas.

---

## Engineering Governance Conflicts

Conflicts involving implementation decisions that may affect governance requirements.

---

## AI Governance Conflicts

Conflicts involving AI recommendations, automation decisions or governance boundaries.

---

## Security Conflicts

Conflicts involving protection of information, systems or institutional assets.

---

# Conflict Detection

Governance conflicts may be identified by:

- Governance Councils;
- Company leadership;
- Engineering teams;
- Audit processes;
- Authorized AI systems;
- Repository reviews;
- Governance pipeline activities.

Every detected conflict shall be documented before resolution activities begin.

---

# Conflict Registration

Every governance conflict shall include:

- Conflict Identifier;
- Date detected;
- Reporting authority;
- Description;
- Affected documents;
- Affected entities;
- Initial classification;
- Responsible review authority.

---

# Resolution Process

Every governance conflict shall follow the following lifecycle:
Conflict Detection

↓

Conflict Registration

↓

Evidence Collection

↓

Authority Identification

↓

Impact Analysis

↓

Resolution Review

↓

Human Decision

↓

Documentation Update

↓

Verification

↓

Historical Record

No stage may be skipped unless an approved emergency governance procedure authorizes an exception.

---

# Initial Resolution

Conflicts shall first be reviewed at the lowest competent governance level.

Escalation shall occur only when:

- the authority level lacks competence;
- the conflict affects higher governance levels;
- no resolution is achieved;
- constitutional interpretation is required.

---

# Escalation Model

Escalation shall follow the Governance Chain defined by the Authority Model.

The escalation path shall preserve:

- authority traceability;
- evidence history;
- previous decisions;
- impact analysis.

Constitutional conflicts shall always be escalated to the BHG Governance Council.

---

# Evidence Review

Every conflict resolution process shall consider:

- applicable governance documents;
- authority definitions;
- historical decisions;
- technical analysis;
- operational impact;
- legal considerations;
- risk assessment.

Opinions without supporting evidence shall not determine governance outcomes.

---

# Legal Hierarchy Application

When conflicting governance documents exist, resolution shall follow the Legal Hierarchy:

1. Higher governance authority prevails.
2. More specific document prevails when authority level is equal.
3. Latest approved version prevails when scope and authority are equivalent.
4. Governance authority resolves unresolved ambiguity.

---

# Artificial Intelligence Role

Authorized AI systems may:

- identify inconsistencies;
- compare documents;
- detect conflicts;
- analyze dependencies;
- simulate possible outcomes;
- prepare resolution reports;
- recommend alternatives.

AI systems shall never:

- resolve conflicts autonomously;
- approve resolutions;
- modify governance authority;
- override human decisions.

---

# Resolution Record

Every resolved conflict shall generate an official record containing:

- Conflict ID;
- Resolution ID;
- Decision authority;
- Resolution summary;
- Supporting evidence;
- Affected documents;
- Required changes;
- Effective date;
- Audit reference.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every governance conflict shall be evaluated as a potential improvement opportunity.

Repeated conflicts may indicate:

- unclear governance;
- insufficient documentation;
- missing standards;
- architectural weaknesses.

Governance maturity shall improve through controlled evolution.

---

# Compliance

Any governance conflict resolved outside this process shall be considered a governance non-conformity.

Such cases shall be reviewed according to the Governance Compliance framework.

---

# Final Principle

Governance does not exist to eliminate disagreement.

Governance exists to ensure that disagreement is resolved through authority, evidence, transparency and institutional integrity.
