# 1. Purpose

This document defines how governance conflicts shall be identified, analyzed, escalated and resolved throughout the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Governance conflicts are considered opportunities to improve institutional clarity rather than organizational failures.

---

# 2. Core Principles

Conflict resolution shall always be:

- Constitutional
- Objective
- Evidence-based
- Human-governed
- Transparent
- Traceable
- Proportional

---

# 3. Types of Governance Conflicts

Governance conflicts may include:

- Authority conflicts
- Policy conflicts
- Constitutional interpretation
- Jurisdiction conflicts
- Engineering conflicts
- AI governance conflicts
- Security conflicts
- Cross-company conflicts

---

# 4. Conflict Detection

Conflicts may be identified by:

- Governance Councils
- Company leaders
- Engineering teams
- Audit processes
- BEiA analysis
- Pull Request reviews

Every identified conflict shall be documented.

---

# 5. Initial Resolution

Conflicts shall first be resolved at the lowest competent governance level.

Escalation is not the default solution.

---

# 6. Escalation

If no agreement is reached, the conflict shall be escalated to the next governance authority according to the Authority Matrix.

Constitutional matters shall always be escalated to the BHG Governance Council.

---

# 7. Evidence Review

Every conflict shall include:

- Relevant documents
- Applicable standards
- Historical decisions
- Technical analysis
- Business impact
- Risk assessment

Opinions without evidence shall not determine governance outcomes.

---

# 8. Constitutional Interpretation

When multiple interpretations exist, the interpretation that best preserves the Constitutional Principles of Breto's Holding Group shall prevail.

---

# 9. Role of BEiA

BEiA may:

- detect inconsistencies
- identify conflicting documents
- simulate outcomes
- prepare impact reports
- recommend alternatives

BEiA shall never resolve governance conflicts autonomously.

Only authorized human authorities may issue final resolutions.

---

# 10. Decision Record

Every resolved conflict shall generate:

- Conflict ID
- Resolution ID
- Decision authority
- Supporting evidence
- Affected documents
- Effective date

---

# 11. Continuous Improvement

Every conflict shall be analyzed to determine whether governance should evolve.

Repeated conflicts indicate governance weaknesses.

Governance shall improve continuously.

---

# 12. Final Principle

The objective of governance is not to eliminate disagreement.

The objective is to resolve disagreement through documented authority, evidence and institutional integrity.
