# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official governance escalation model used throughout the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Its objective is to ensure that every decision is taken by the appropriate governance authority.

Escalation exists to improve decision quality, not to transfer responsibility unnecessarily.

---

# 2. Core Principles

Governance escalation shall always be:

- Necessary
- Proportional
- Transparent
- Traceable
- Evidence-based
- Human-governed

---

# 3. Escalation Philosophy

Every governance level should solve as many decisions as possible.

Escalation is an exception.

Not the default workflow.

---

# 4. Escalation Levels

Level 1

Project Team

Level 2

Department

Level 3

Company Governance

Level 4

SubHolding Governance

Level 5

Breto's Holding Group Governance Council

Each level owns decisions within its defined authority.

---

# 5. Escalation Triggers

Escalation may occur when:

- authority is insufficient
- constitutional interpretation is required
- cross-company impact exists
- financial exposure exceeds delegated authority
- security impact exceeds local authority
- legal implications exist
- strategic impact affects the ecosystem

---

# 6. Escalation Requirements

Every escalation shall include:

- Decision summary
- Scope
- Business justification
- Technical analysis
- Risk analysis
- Supporting evidence
- Recommended alternatives

---

# 7. Escalation Timing

Escalations shall occur as early as reasonably possible.

Late escalation increases organizational risk.

---

# 8. Role of BEiA

BEiA may:

- prepare escalation reports
- summarize evidence
- compare alternatives
- estimate impacts
- identify affected governance documents

BEiA never escalates decisions autonomously.

Escalation is initiated by authorized humans.

---

# 9. Constitutional Escalation

Matters affecting:

- Constitutional principles
- Governance structure
- Authority model
- Corporate identity
- Holding-wide standards

shall always be escalated to the BHG Governance Council.

---

# 10. Resolution

The receiving governance authority shall:

- accept
- reject
- return for additional information
- delegate appropriately

Every outcome shall be documented.

---

# 11. Repository Traceability

Every escalation shall generate:

- Escalation ID
- Decision ID
- Repository reference
- Version history
- Responsible authority
- Resolution record

---

# 12. Continuous Improvement

Repeated escalations of the same type indicate governance gaps.

Those gaps should trigger governance evolution proposals.

---

# 13. Final Principle

Authority should remain as close as possible to execution, while constitutional coherence remains centralized.
