# Enterprise Lifecycle

## Purpose

This document defines the official lifecycle of every enterprise operating within Breto's Holding Group.

The objective is to ensure that every company evolves through a predictable, auditable and governance-driven process.

No enterprise may exist outside this lifecycle.

---

# Principles

Every enterprise shall:

- exist for a defined purpose
- contribute to the ecosystem
- evolve through measurable stages
- maintain governance compatibility
- remain aligned with the Holding Constitution

---

# Enterprise Lifecycle

Every enterprise progresses through the following stages.

## Stage 1 — Idea

The enterprise exists only as a strategic proposal.

Activities include:

- opportunity analysis
- market validation
- risk identification
- alignment with Holding strategy

No operational activities are authorized.

---

## Stage 2 — Incubation

The enterprise begins structured development.

Typical activities include:

- governance creation
- documentation
- architecture
- MVP planning
- legal analysis

Products may be prototyped.

Commercial operations remain limited.

---

## Stage 3 — Foundation

The enterprise becomes formally established.

Requirements include:

- approved constitution
- governance model
- executive structure
- operational roadmap
- legal registration (when applicable)

The company becomes an official ecosystem member.

---

## Stage 4 — Growth

The enterprise expands operations.

Typical activities include:

- customer acquisition
- product expansion
- hiring
- partnerships
- infrastructure scaling

Governance maturity increases continuously.

---

## Stage 5 — Consolidation

The enterprise operates as a stable and sustainable organization.

Characteristics include:

- predictable operations
- mature governance
- financial sustainability
- organizational stability
- strategic autonomy

---

## Stage 6 — Expansion

The enterprise expands beyond its original market.

Expansion may include:

- international operations
- new business units
- acquisitions
- subsidiaries
- strategic alliances

---

## Stage 7 — Transformation

The enterprise undergoes significant structural change.

Examples include:

- mergers
- spin-offs
- restructuring
- technology replacement
- governance redesign

Transformation shall preserve governance continuity whenever possible.

---

## Stage 8 — Legacy

The enterprise no longer prioritizes growth.

Instead it focuses on:

- preserving knowledge
- mentoring new companies
- long-term stewardship
- ecosystem stability

Legacy companies become institutional assets.

---

# Enterprise State Transitions

Progression between stages requires formal approval according to governance authority.

Transitions shall always be documented.

Skipped stages are prohibited unless explicitly authorized.

---

# Governance Requirements

Each stage requires increasing governance maturity.

Operational complexity shall never exceed governance maturity.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Each lifecycle transition shall include:

- updated documentation
- governance review
- architectural review
- strategic justification
- traceability records

---

# AI Support

BEiA may:

- evaluate readiness
- detect governance gaps
- prepare transition reports
- simulate risks
- recommend improvements

BEiA shall never authorize lifecycle transitions.

Only human governance bodies possess approval authority.

---

# Holding Responsibility

Breto's Holding Group is responsible for maintaining lifecycle consistency across all enterprises.

Individual enterprises remain operationally autonomous while respecting this framework.

---

# Compliance

Every enterprise shall continuously demonstrate compliance with this lifecycle.

Non-compliance shall trigger governance review.

---

# Continuous Evolution

This lifecycle may evolve through the official governance evolution process defined by the Holding.
