# Project Model

# Purpose

This document defines the official project model used throughout the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Projects are the controlled vehicles through which change is introduced into the ecosystem.

Projects create, improve, transform or retire enterprises, companies, products, services, platforms, processes and governance assets.

---

# Definition

A Project is a temporary organizational initiative created to achieve one or more predefined objectives within an approved scope.

Unlike enterprises, products or services, projects have a defined beginning and a defined end.

Projects exist only until their objectives have been completed or formally terminated.

---

# Core Principles

Every project shall be:

- intentional
- temporary
- governed
- documented
- measurable
- traceable
- auditable

No project shall exist without governance.

---

# Purpose of Projects

Projects exist to generate controlled change.

They may create:

- enterprises
- companies
- products
- services
- infrastructure
- software
- governance
- documentation
- operational capabilities

Projects do not exist merely to perform routine operational work.

---

# Project Characteristics

Every project shall have:

- a clear objective
- defined scope
- expected outcomes
- responsible owner
- approved governance
- measurable success criteria
- documented lifecycle

---

# Project Classification

Projects may be classified as:

## Strategic Projects

Initiatives that significantly affect the ecosystem.

Examples:

- creation of a new enterprise
- creation of a subholding
- ecosystem transformation

---

## Business Projects

Projects focused on commercial value.

Examples:

- new financial products
- commercial expansion
- partnerships

---

## Technology Projects

Projects focused on engineering.

Examples:

- software platforms
- infrastructure
- cybersecurity
- AI systems

---

## Governance Projects

Projects intended to improve organizational governance.

Examples:

- constitutions
- standards
- operating models
- compliance frameworks

---

## Research Projects

Projects intended to validate ideas.

Examples:

- prototypes
- proof of concepts
- experimentation

---

# Project Ownership

Every project shall have:

- Project Sponsor
- Project Owner
- Responsible Enterprise
- Governance Authority

Responsibilities shall be documented.

---

# Human Authority

Strategic decisions shall always remain under human authority.

BEiA may provide:

- impact analysis
- planning recommendations
- dependency analysis
- risk simulations
- governance validation support

BEiA shall never approve projects.

Only authorized human governance bodies may approve project creation, suspension or closure.

---

# Relationships

Projects may create or modify:

- enterprises
- companies
- products
- services
- documentation
- platforms
- processes

Projects shall never replace operational ownership.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful project delivers:

- defined objectives
- measurable value
- complete documentation
- governance compliance
- operational readiness
- traceability

---

# Compliance

Every project within the BHG ecosystem shall comply with this model.

Exceptions require formal approval by the Governance Council.
