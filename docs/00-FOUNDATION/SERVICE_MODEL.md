# Service Model

# Purpose

This document defines the official service model adopted across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

A service represents an operational capability delivered by an enterprise to internal or external consumers.

Services are reusable business capabilities.

Products package services.

Companies operate services.

The Holding governs the model.

---

# Definition

A Service is a repeatable business capability designed to deliver value to one or more consumers.

A service may be:

- internal
- external
- commercial
- operational
- technical

A service is not necessarily a product.

Several services may compose a single product.

---

# Service Principles

Every service shall be:

- reusable
- documented
- governed
- measurable
- secure
- versioned
- scalable
- traceable
- maintainable

---

# Service Ownership

Every service shall have:

- one responsible enterprise
- one service owner
- one documented purpose
- one lifecycle
- one version history

Ownership shall always be explicit.

---

# Service Classification

Services may belong to one or more categories.

## Business Services

Capabilities directly consumed by customers.

Examples:

- Identity Verification
- Payment Processing
- Financial Profile

---

## Platform Services

Capabilities supporting products.

Examples:

- Authentication
- Notifications
- File Storage

---

## Infrastructure Services

Shared technical capabilities.

Examples:

- Logging
- Monitoring
- Security
- Backup

---

## AI Services

Capabilities provided by AI systems.

Examples:

- Evidence Analysis
- Risk Assessment
- Document Classification

AI services remain advisory.

Human authority remains mandatory.

---

# Relationship with Products

Products consume services.

Services may support multiple products simultaneously.

Products define customer experiences.

Services define operational capabilities.

---

# Relationship with Enterprises

Each enterprise may own multiple services.

A service shall always belong to a single responsible enterprise.

Cross-enterprise collaboration is permitted through documented governance agreements.

---

# Lifecycle

Every service shall follow the official Service Lifecycle.

No service shall exist without lifecycle governance.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Every service shall document:

- purpose
- owner
- consumers
- interfaces
- dependencies
- risks
- KPIs
- version history

---

# Security

Every service shall comply with:

- Engineering Standards
- Security Standards
- AI Governance
- Documentation Standards

Security shall be designed before implementation.

---

# Metrics

Every service should define measurable indicators including:

- availability
- reliability
- response time
- adoption
- customer value
- operational cost

---

# AI Participation

Artificial Intelligence may:

- recommend improvements
- analyze performance
- identify risks
- generate reports

Artificial Intelligence shall never replace human accountability.

---

# Governance

Services shall evolve through documented governance processes.

Major changes require documented approval.

Breaking changes shall follow versioning policies.

---

# Compliance

Every enterprise within the ecosystem shall adopt this Service Model before introducing operational or commercial services.
