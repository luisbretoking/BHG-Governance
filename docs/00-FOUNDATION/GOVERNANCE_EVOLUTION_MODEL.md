# Governance Evolution Model

> Defines the official lifecycle through which governance evolves while preserving institutional stability.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance evolution model adopted by Breto's Holding Group.

Its objective is to ensure that governance evolves in a predictable, transparent and auditable manner without compromising constitutional integrity or organizational continuity.

---

# Evolution Principle

Governance is designed to evolve.

Its evolution shall always preserve institutional knowledge, traceability and normative consistency.

---

# Evolution Objectives

Governance evolution shall:

- Improve institutional effectiveness.
- Preserve constitutional integrity.
- Reduce governance ambiguity.
- Enable sustainable innovation.
- Strengthen organizational resilience.

---

# Evolution Triggers

Governance evolution may be initiated by:

- Constitutional review.
- Organizational growth.
- Regulatory changes.
- Architectural evolution.
- Engineering improvements.
- Audit findings.
- Risk assessments.
- Strategic initiatives.

Every trigger shall be documented.

---

# Evolution Lifecycle

Governance evolution follows the following lifecycle:

1. Identification
2. Proposal
3. Impact Assessment
4. Technical Review
5. Governance Review
6. Approval
7. Implementation
8. Verification
9. Institutionalization

Each stage shall preserve documentary evidence.

---

# Governance Constraints

Governance evolution shall never:

- Contradict the Constitution.
- Break governance hierarchy.
- Eliminate traceability.
- Remove historical evidence.
- Introduce conflicting authorities.

---

# Impact Assessment

Every governance change shall evaluate:

- Constitutional impact.
- Organizational impact.
- Architectural impact.
- Dependency impact.
- Compliance impact.
- Automation impact.

High-impact changes require formal governance approval.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Every governance evolution shall identify:

- Motivation.
- Scope.
- Expected benefits.
- Risks.
- Dependencies.
- Affected documents.
- Responsible authority.

---

# Governance Continuity

Governance evolution shall preserve:

- Version history.
- Decision rationale.
- Authority chain.
- Documentary relationships.
- Knowledge continuity.

Institutional memory shall never be lost.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Governance evolution is a continuous process.

Periodic reviews shall identify opportunities to improve clarity, efficiency and long-term sustainability.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Stable governance enables sustainable evolution.

> Controlled evolution preserves institutional knowledge while enabling continuous innovation.
