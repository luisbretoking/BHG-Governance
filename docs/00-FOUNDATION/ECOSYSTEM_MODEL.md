# Ecosystem Model

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Official

Owner: Breto's Holding Group Governance Council

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official organizational structure of the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

It establishes how every corporate entity relates to one another while preserving governance consistency, operational autonomy and long-term scalability.

---

# 2. Principles

The ecosystem shall operate under the following principles:

- One Constitution.
- Distributed execution.
- Local autonomy.
- Global alignment.
- Shared governance.
- Shared engineering.
- Shared trust.
- Shared identity.
- Independent innovation.
- Complete traceability.

---

# 3. Ecosystem Layers

The ecosystem is organized into multiple governance layers.

Layer 0

Breto's Holding Group

Corporate governance.

Vision.

Capital allocation.

Corporate strategy.

Institutional governance.

Global standards.

---

Layer 1

SubHoldings

Each SubHolding governs one strategic industry.

Examples:

Financial Infrastructure

Media

Education

Health

Energy

Mobility

Real Estate

Technology

Industrial Services

Each SubHolding owns its own governance framework while remaining constitutionally aligned with BHG.

---

Layer 2

Companies

Each company belongs to exactly one SubHolding.

Companies possess operational autonomy.

They may publish internal constitutions.

Internal policies.

Engineering standards.

Operating procedures.

Provided they never violate BHG governance.

---

Layer 3

Platforms

A company may own one or more platforms.

Examples:

Payment platforms

Identity platforms

Cloud platforms

AI platforms

Developer platforms

Data platforms

---

Layer 4

Products

Products represent customer-facing solutions.

Examples:

Mobile Apps

Web Applications

APIs

SDKs

Cards

Identity Wallets

Internal Systems

---

Layer 5

Services

Products may expose one or more services.

Examples:

Payments

Identity Verification

Document Verification

Credit Analysis

AI Assistance

Notifications

Analytics

---

Layer 6

Capabilities

Capabilities are reusable business competencies.

Examples

Identity

Risk

Payments

Compliance

Artificial Intelligence

Data Intelligence

Communications

Security

Developer Experience

Capabilities should be reusable across the ecosystem.

---

# 4. Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is considered a transversal capability.

AI does not constitute an authority layer.

AI supports every layer.

AI never replaces governance.

AI never approves policies.

AI never exercises institutional authority.

---

# 5. Shared Infrastructure

Infrastructure should be shared whenever possible.

Examples:

Identity

Authentication

Authorization

Cloud

Logging

Monitoring

AI

Developer Platforms

Documentation

Engineering Standards

Security

---

# 6. Local Autonomy

Every organizational unit may create:

Policies

Standards

Processes

Procedures

Playbooks

Engineering rules

provided they remain compatible with BHG governance.

---

# 7. Governance Alignment

Every entity shall maintain alignment with:

Mission

Vision

Constitution

Governance Model

Authority Model

Engineering Constitution

AI Constitution

---

# 8. Evolution

The ecosystem is expected to evolve continuously.

New industries.

New companies.

New technologies.

New business models.

The governance architecture must enable expansion without structural redesign.

---

# 9. Human Authority

Strategic authority always belongs to humans.

AI acts only as an intelligence system.

Decision support.

Impact analysis.

Simulation.

Risk identification.

Documentation assistance.

Final authority remains human.

---

# 10. Traceability

Every ecosystem element shall possess:

Owner

Purpose

Authority

Dependencies

Version

Lifecycle

Relationship to other entities

All relationships must remain traceable.
