# 1. Purpose

The Governance Council is the highest institutional governance authority within Breto's Holding Group.

Its mission is to preserve the integrity, coherence, sustainability and long-term evolution of the corporate governance framework.

The Council exists to protect the ecosystem rather than manage daily operations.

---

# 2. Scope

The Governance Council oversees:

- Corporate governance
- Constitutional evolution
- Governance policies
- Corporate standards
- Institutional principles
- Governance conflicts
- Cross-company governance alignment

The Council does not manage operational activities.

---

# 3. Core Responsibilities

The Council shall:

- Protect the Constitution.
- Maintain governance consistency.
- Approve governance amendments.
- Resolve governance conflicts.
- Protect long-term institutional vision.
- Preserve ecosystem harmony.
- Promote sustainable evolution.

---

# 4. Human Authority

The Governance Council is composed exclusively of human members.

Institutional authority belongs only to designated human representatives.

Human accountability cannot be delegated.

---

# 5. Role of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence supports governance.

Artificial Intelligence does not govern.

BEiA may:

- analyze proposals;
- simulate impacts;
- detect inconsistencies;
- identify conflicts;
- prepare evidence;
- recommend alternatives;
- generate documentation;
- assess traceability.

BEiA shall never:

- approve governance;
- reject governance;
- vote;
- exercise institutional authority;
- replace human accountability.

All AI recommendations remain advisory.

---

# 6. Governance Decisions

Governance decisions shall be based on:

- Evidence
- Strategic alignment
- Long-term sustainability
- Risk assessment
- Ecosystem impact

Opinion alone is insufficient.

---

# 7. Governance Meetings

Meetings may occur:

- Regularly
- Extraordinary
- Emergency

Meeting procedures shall be documented.

---

# 8. Decision Records

Every official decision shall record:

- Decision ID
- Proposal ID
- Participants
- Evidence
- Final Decision
- Approval Date
- Effective Date
- Version Impact

No official governance decision may remain undocumented.

---

# 9. Relationship with SubHoldings

SubHoldings maintain governance autonomy.

SubHoldings may establish additional governance according to their business context.

Local governance shall remain compatible with mandatory corporate governance.

Corporate governance establishes minimum requirements.

SubHoldings may strengthen governance but shall not weaken corporate governance.

---

# 10. Governance Harmony

The Council shall preserve governance harmony across the ecosystem.

Whenever governance conflicts arise, solutions shall prioritize ecosystem coherence over local optimization.

---

# 11. Continuous Evolution

The Governance Council encourages continuous improvement.

Every company, department, project and employee may formally propose governance improvements.

Constructive evolution is considered an institutional responsibility.

---

# 12. Compliance

All governance decisions approved by the Council become mandatory according to the Legal Hierarchy.

---

# 13. Exceptions

Only the Governance Council may authorize governance exceptions.

Every exception shall be documented, justified and reviewed.
