# Document Lifecycle

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Active

Owner: BHG Governance Council

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official lifecycle of every governance document within Breto's Holding Group.

Its objective is to ensure that every document:

- has a clear origin;
- has an accountable owner;
- evolves through controlled governance;
- remains auditable;
- may eventually be replaced or retired without creating inconsistencies.

No governance document exists outside this lifecycle.

---

# Lifecycle Stages

Every official document shall belong to one of the following stages.

## 1. Proposal

The document is only an idea.

Characteristics:

- no authority
- draft only
- open discussion
- no implementation required

---

## 2. Draft

The document has begun formal development.

Characteristics:

- version numbering starts
- governance review begins
- may receive Pull Requests
- not yet mandatory

---

## 3. Review

The document is under official governance evaluation.

Activities include:

- technical review
- legal review
- architectural review
- AI analysis
- impact simulation
- dependency analysis

No implementation shall occur during this stage.

---

## 4. Approved

The Governance Council approves the document.

Effects:

- becomes official
- receives governance authority
- may govern other documents
- implementation becomes authorized

---

## 5. Active

The document is actively governing the organization.

Requirements:

- version controlled
- traceable
- periodically reviewed
- publicly referenced inside repositories

---

## 6. Deprecated

The document is no longer recommended.

Characteristics:

- remains available
- historical value preserved
- new work should migrate elsewhere

Deprecation never deletes history.

---

## 7. Archived

The document has historical value only.

Characteristics:

- immutable
- read-only
- retained permanently

Archived documents preserve institutional memory.

---

## 8. Superseded

The document has been replaced.

The replacement document shall always be referenced.

Example:

Superseded by:

DOCUMENT_X.md

---

## 9. Retired

The document no longer governs any activity.

Retirement requires:

- Governance Council approval
- migration completed
- dependency verification
- traceability preserved

Retired documents remain part of organizational history.

---

# Mandatory Metadata

Every governance document shall contain:

Version

Status

Owner

Authority

Dependencies

Last Review

Next Review

Related Documents

---

# Ownership

Every document shall have one responsible owner.

Ownership may belong to:

- Governance Council
- Engineering Council
- AI Council
- Security Council
- Company Governance Board

Ownership never belongs to an AI.

---

# Review Frequency

Default review interval:

12 months

Critical documents:

6 months

Emergency reviews:

Immediate

---

# AI Participation

BEiA may:

- analyze impacts
- detect inconsistencies
- simulate changes
- generate comparisons
- recommend improvements

BEiA shall never:

- approve documents
- reject documents
- change authority
- vote
- become document owner

Human governance remains the final authority.

---

# Traceability

Every lifecycle transition shall include:

Version

Commit

Pull Request

Decision Record

Approver

Date

Reason

Impact Summary

No undocumented transition is valid.

---

# Compatibility

All subsidiaries shall adopt compatible lifecycle models.

They may extend this lifecycle but shall never contradict it.

---

# Guiding Principle

Governance documents are living institutional assets.

They evolve through evidence,
remain controlled through governance,
and preserve organizational memory forever.
