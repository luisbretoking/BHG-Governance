# Governance Pipeline

> Official governance lifecycle framework of Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

The Governance Pipeline defines the official lifecycle followed by every governance proposal within the BHG ecosystem.

Its purpose is to guarantee that governance evolves through:

- evidence;
- traceability;
- controlled review;
- human authority;
- documented implementation.

No governance artifact may be created, modified or removed outside this pipeline unless an approved emergency governance process explicitly authorizes the exception.

---

# Core Principles

## Governance Changes Begin With Proposals

Every governance modification shall originate from a documented proposal.

---

## Unique Identification

Every governance proposal shall receive a unique identifier.

---

## Full Traceability

Every governance action shall preserve:

- origin;
- responsible parties;
- review history;
- approval decision;
- implementation evidence.

---

## AI Assistance Principle

Artificial Intelligence systems may support governance analysis.

AI systems shall never replace governance authority.

---

## Human Approval Authority

Only authorized human governance bodies may approve official governance changes.

---

---

# Pipeline Stages

## Stage 1 — Idea

A governance opportunity, problem or improvement opportunity is identified.

Possible sources include:

- employees;
- managers;
- executives;
- governance committees;
- authorized AI systems.

---

## Stage 2 — Governance Proposal

The idea is transformed into a formal governance proposal according to the Governance Proposal Standard.

---

## Stage 3 — Initial Validation

The responsible authority verifies:

- completeness;
- scope;
- relevance;
- affected governance artifacts.

---

## Stage 4 — Department Review

The responsible organizational area reviews the proposal.

Local improvements may be resolved at the appropriate authority level.

Strategic proposals continue through the pipeline.

---

## Stage 5 — AI Analysis

Authorized AI systems may perform:

- consistency analysis;
- dependency analysis;
- conflict detection;
- duplicate detection;
- historical comparison;
- compliance verification;
- impact simulation.

AI output is advisory only.

---

## Stage 6 — Impact Assessment

The proposal impact shall be documented.

Evaluation areas may include:

- governance;
- engineering;
- operations;
- legal;
- documentation;
- technology.

---

## Stage 7 — Risk Assessment

Potential risks shall be identified and evaluated.

Including:

- governance risks;
- operational risks;
- security risks;
- legal risks;
- implementation risks.

---

## Stage 8 — Governance Review

The appropriate governance authority evaluates the complete proposal package.

Additional evidence may be requested.

---

## Stage 9 — Approval Decision

Authorized human governance bodies may:

- approve;
- reject;
- defer;
- request modifications.

Every decision shall be recorded.

---

## Stage 10 — Implementation

Approved governance changes are implemented.

Version history and traceability shall be preserved.

---

## Stage 11 — Verification

The implementation shall be verified against the approved proposal.

---

## Stage 12 — Governance Audit

Auditors and authorized AI systems may verify:

- compliance;
- traceability;
- documentation;
- implementation consistency.

---

## Stage 13 — Continuous Monitoring

Governance effectiveness shall be monitored continuously.

Detected improvements may generate new proposals.

---

# Artificial Intelligence Responsibilities

AI systems may:

- monitor repositories;
- detect inconsistencies;
- validate governance rules;
- generate reports;
- recommend improvements;
- prepare proposal drafts.

AI systems shall never:

- approve governance;
- reject governance;
- modify official governance artifacts directly;
- bypass governance authority.

---

# Emergency Governance

Emergency procedures may accelerate governance review.

Emergency procedures shall never eliminate:

- traceability;
- human approval;
- auditability.

---

# Governance As Code

The Governance Pipeline shall remain machine-readable.

Authorized automation may:

- verify lifecycle completion;
- validate approvals;
- detect missing evidence;
- monitor compliance.

Automation supports governance.

Human authority governs governance.

---

# Compliance

Any governance modification executed outside this pipeline shall be considered a governance non-conformity.

Such cases shall follow the Governance Conflict Resolution process.
