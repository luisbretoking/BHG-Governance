# Governance Pipeline

## Purpose

This document defines the official lifecycle followed by every governance proposal inside the BHG ecosystem.

The Governance Pipeline guarantees that governance evolves through evidence, traceability, human review and controlled implementation.

No governance artifact may be modified outside this pipeline.

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1

Every governance change begins with a proposal.

---

## Principle 2

Every proposal shall be uniquely identifiable.

---

## Principle 3

Every proposal shall remain fully traceable.

---

## Principle 4

Artificial Intelligence supports the pipeline but never replaces governance authority.

---

## Principle 5

Human governance bodies retain exclusive approval authority.

---

# Governance Pipeline

Every governance proposal shall follow the following lifecycle.

```
Idea
↓

Governance Proposal

↓

Initial Validation

↓

Department Review

↓

AI Analysis

↓

Impact Assessment

↓

Risk Assessment

↓

Governance Review

↓

Approval Decision

↓

Implementation

↓

Verification

↓

Governance Audit

↓

Continuous Monitoring

↓

Closed
```

No stage may be skipped unless an officially approved emergency governance procedure explicitly authorizes it.

---

# Pipeline Stages

## Stage 1 — Idea

A governance opportunity is identified.

The proposer may be:

- Employee
- Manager
- Executive
- Governance Committee
- AI System

---

## Stage 2 — Governance Proposal

A formal proposal is created following the Governance Proposal Standard.

---

## Stage 3 — Initial Validation

The responsible authority verifies:

- completeness
- scope
- relevance
- affected governance artifacts

---

## Stage 4 — Department Review

The proposal is reviewed by the responsible organizational unit.

Minor improvements may be resolved locally.

Strategic proposals continue through the pipeline.

---

## Stage 5 — AI Analysis

Authorized AI systems may perform:

- consistency analysis

- dependency analysis

- conflict detection

- duplicate detection

- historical comparison

- compliance verification

- impact simulation

AI analysis is advisory only.

---

## Stage 6 — Impact Assessment

Expected organizational impacts are documented.

Including:

- governance

- engineering

- operations

- legal

- documentation

- technology

---

## Stage 7 — Risk Assessment

Potential risks shall be identified.

Including:

- governance risks

- operational risks

- security risks

- legal risks

- implementation risks

---

## Stage 8 — Governance Review

The appropriate governance authority reviews the complete proposal package.

Additional evidence may be requested.

---

## Stage 9 — Approval Decision

Only authorized human governance bodies may:

- approve

- reject

- defer

- request modifications

Every decision shall be documented.

---

## Stage 10 — Implementation

Approved governance changes are implemented.

Version history shall be preserved.

---

## Stage 11 — Verification

Implementation is verified against the approved proposal.

---

## Stage 12 — Governance Audit

Authorized auditors and AI systems verify:

- compliance

- traceability

- documentation

- implementation consistency

---

## Stage 13 — Continuous Monitoring

Governance effectiveness shall be continuously monitored.

Future improvements may generate new governance proposals.

---

# AI Responsibilities

AI systems may:

- monitor repositories

- detect inconsistencies

- validate governance rules

- generate reports

- recommend improvements

- prepare proposal drafts

AI systems shall never:

- approve governance

- reject governance

- modify governance artifacts directly

- bypass the Governance Chain

---

# Emergency Governance

Emergency procedures may accelerate reviews.

Emergency procedures shall never eliminate:

- traceability

- human approval

- auditability

---

# Governance as Code

Every pipeline stage shall be machine-readable.

Governance automation may:

- verify stage completion

- validate approvals

- detect missing evidence

- monitor compliance

Automation supports governance.

Human authority governs governance.

---

# Compliance

Any governance modification executed outside this pipeline shall be considered a governance non-conformity.

Such cases shall follow the Governance Conflict Resolution process.
