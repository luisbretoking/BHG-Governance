# Governance Interaction Model

**Document ID:** BHG-GOV-INTERACTION

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Active

---

# Purpose

This document defines how governance authorities interact across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Its objective is to guarantee coordination without reducing organizational autonomy.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Good governance is not created by concentrating power.

Good governance is created by coordinating responsibilities.

Every governance body exists for a different purpose.

---

# Interaction Principles

All governance interactions shall follow these principles:

- Respect constitutional authority.
- Respect organizational autonomy.
- Share evidence.
- Maintain transparency.
- Preserve traceability.
- Escalate only when necessary.

---

# Governance Bodies

The ecosystem may include:

- BHG Governance Council
- Company Governance Councils
- Engineering Councils
- AI Governance Committees
- Security Committees
- Architecture Boards
- Risk Committees
- Ethics Committees

Additional governance bodies may be created whenever necessary.

---

# Collaboration Rule

Governance bodies collaborate.

They do not compete.

Whenever two governance bodies share responsibilities they shall coordinate before making decisions.

---

# Escalation Rule

Operational decisions remain operational.

Strategic decisions remain strategic.

Constitutional decisions remain constitutional.

Only unresolved conflicts shall be escalated.

---

# Information Exchange

Governance bodies shall exchange:

- reports
- evidence
- impact analysis
- risk assessments
- recommendations
- implementation status

Information shall remain version controlled.

---

# Decision Transparency

Every governance decision shall include:

- decision owner
- scope
- rationale
- supporting evidence
- affected documents
- implementation plan

---

# Role of BEiA

BEiA facilitates governance interaction by:

- collecting evidence
- identifying conflicts
- generating comparisons
- simulating impacts
- preparing documentation

BEiA shall never coordinate governance independently.

Only humans coordinate governance.

---

# Cross-Company Collaboration

Companies are encouraged to collaborate.

Shared initiatives shall respect:

- local autonomy
- constitutional governance
- shared standards

No company may impose governance over another company.

---

# Conflict Resolution

Conflicts shall be resolved using:

1. Dialogue.
2. Evidence.
3. Governance review.
4. Constitutional interpretation.

Personal authority shall never replace documented governance.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Governance interaction shall continuously evolve.

Lessons learned shall be documented.

Successful practices should be standardized.

---

# Final Principle

Strong governance is measured by the quality of collaboration, not by the concentration of authority.
