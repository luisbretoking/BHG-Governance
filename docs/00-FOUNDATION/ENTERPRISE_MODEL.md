# Enterprise Model

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official enterprise architecture of Breto's Holding Group.

It establishes what an enterprise is, how enterprises are classified, how they interact with the Holding, and which governance principles every enterprise must permanently respect.

It creates a common language that allows every future company to be built under the same institutional architecture.

---

# 2. Core Principle

Every enterprise exists to fulfill a specific mission while contributing to the long-term mission of Breto's Holding Group.

Enterprises are autonomous.

The ecosystem is aligned.

---

# 3. Enterprise Definition

An Enterprise is an officially recognized organizational entity operating inside the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

An enterprise may own:

- products
- services
- intellectual property
- technology
- assets
- operations
- employees
- governance documents

Every enterprise has its own identity.

No enterprise exists outside governance.

---

# 4. Enterprise Categories

The ecosystem may include multiple organizational levels.

## Holding

Owns the ecosystem.

Defines constitutional governance.

Protects long-term vision.

Coordinates strategic alignment.

Never performs operational micromanagement.

---

## Subholding

Groups companies operating in the same strategic domain.

Examples:

- Financial Infrastructure
- Digital Identity
- Media
- Artificial Intelligence
- Cloud Infrastructure

A Subholding may govern multiple companies.

---

## Company

A legally or operationally independent organization.

Responsible for delivering products and services.

Owns operational governance.

May create internal policies.

Must remain aligned with Holding governance.

---

## Business Unit

A specialized operational division.

Examples:

- Engineering
- Operations
- Finance
- Compliance
- Product
- Security
- AI

Business Units execute work.

They do not redefine governance.

---

## Team

Small execution groups responsible for specific objectives.

---

# 5. Enterprise Independence

Every enterprise has operational autonomy.

Operational autonomy includes:

- internal organization
- hiring
- engineering practices
- technology choices
- operational processes
- budgeting
- execution

Operational autonomy never overrides constitutional governance.

---

# 6. Governance Alignment

Every enterprise shall remain aligned with:

- Constitution
- Governance Model
- Authority Model
- Legal Hierarchy
- Mandatory Foundation Policies

Local governance may extend global governance.

Local governance shall never contradict global governance.

---

# 7. Local Governance

Every enterprise may create:

- policies
- standards
- procedures
- workflows
- operational rules
- technical documentation

These documents govern only that enterprise unless officially adopted by the Holding.

---

# 8. Enterprise Responsibilities

Every enterprise is responsible for:

- protecting the ecosystem
- maintaining documentation
- ensuring traceability
- managing risks
- complying with governance
- protecting intellectual property
- promoting innovation
- supporting interoperability

---

# 9. Relationship with AI

AI systems support enterprises.

AI may:

- analyze
- simulate
- review
- recommend
- monitor
- document

AI shall never replace institutional authority.

Human governance remains final.

---

# 10. Enterprise Lifecycle

Every enterprise follows an official lifecycle:

Idea

↓

Proposal

↓

Evaluation

↓

Approval

↓

Formation

↓

Operation

↓

Growth

↓

Transformation

↓

Merger

or

Closure

Every transition shall be documented.

---

# 11. Enterprise Identity

Each enterprise owns:

- mission
- vision
- governance
- documentation
- architecture
- operating model
- branding
- products
- roadmap

Identity belongs to the enterprise.

Purpose belongs to the Holding.

---

# 12. Enterprise Collaboration

Enterprises collaborate through:

- shared standards
- shared architecture
- shared governance
- shared services
- shared technology
- shared knowledge

Competition inside the ecosystem is prohibited.

Collaboration is mandatory.

---

# 13. Enterprise Evolution

Enterprises are expected to evolve.

Governance exists to enable sustainable evolution.

Innovation shall always remain compatible with institutional integrity.

---

# 14. Enterprise Success

Success is evaluated by more than financial performance.

The Holding considers:

- sustainability
- customer trust
- governance maturity
- documentation quality
- technological excellence
- innovation
- interoperability
- long-term value creation
- social impact

---

# 15. Final Principle

Strong enterprises create strong ecosystems.

Strong ecosystems create enduring institutions.

Every enterprise contributes to the legacy of Breto's Holding Group.
