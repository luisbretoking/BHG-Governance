# Governance Approval Model

> Defines how governance decisions are proposed, reviewed, approved, delegated and escalated across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

This model establishes the official governance approval framework for the entire BHG ecosystem.

Its objectives are to:

- enable distributed governance;
- preserve constitutional authority;
- eliminate unnecessary approval bottlenecks;
- ensure traceable decision making;
- support continuous organizational evolution.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Governance authority is centralized.

Operational decision making is federated.

Innovation is decentralized.

Constitutional integrity remains protected.

---

# Approval Levels

The governance ecosystem defines five approval levels.

## Level 1 — Constitutional

Responsible for:

- Constitution
- Governance Model
- Legal Hierarchy
- Authority Model

Approval Authority:

BHG Governance Council

---

## Level 2 — Corporate Governance

Responsible for:

- Policies
- Enterprise Standards
- Cross-company governance

Approval Authority:

BHG Governance Council

or

Delegated Enterprise Governance Board.

---

## Level 3 — Company Governance

Responsible for:

- Company Constitutions
- Company Policies
- Company Standards

Approval Authority:

Company Governance Council.

---

## Level 4 — Operational Governance

Responsible for:

- Procedures
- Workflows
- Operational Standards

Approval Authority:

Department Governance Board.

---

## Level 5 — Implementation

Responsible for:

- Software
- Documentation
- Services
- Platforms

Approval Authority:

Engineering leadership.

---

# Federated Approval Principle

Every organizational unit may approve governance artifacts within its delegated authority.

No approval may exceed the authority granted by higher governance levels.

---

# Upward Governance Feedback

Governance proposals may originate at any level.

Improvement proposals shall move upward through the governance hierarchy when broader institutional impact exists.

Bottom-up innovation is encouraged.

Top-down authority is preserved.

---

# AI Analytical Participation

Artificial Intelligence systems participate as governance analysts.

AI systems may:

- detect inconsistencies;
- identify governance conflicts;
- recommend improvements;
- simulate governance impact;
- classify proposals;
- estimate organizational risk.

AI systems shall never approve governance.

---

# Federated Governance Councils

Each company may establish its own Governance Council.

Company Governance Councils may approve:

- company constitutions;
- company standards;
- operational governance;
- internal policies.

These approvals remain valid provided they do not conflict with higher governance authority.

---

# Escalation Rules

Escalation is mandatory when:

- constitutional conflicts exist;
- authority boundaries are exceeded;
- governance ownership is unclear;
- cross-company impact is detected.

---

# Approval Traceability

Every governance approval shall record:

- proposal identifier;
- approving authority;
- approval date;
- rationale;
- impacted documents;
- governance level;
- superseded versions.

---

# Operational Continuity

Federated approvals exist to prevent governance bottlenecks.

The inability of one governance body shall not unnecessarily halt unrelated organizational operations.

Delegated governance preserves operational continuity while maintaining constitutional compliance.

---

# Governance Integrity

Delegated authority shall never weaken constitutional authority.

Every approval remains subject to audit.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Governance scales through delegated authority.

> Constitutional integrity scales through traceability.
