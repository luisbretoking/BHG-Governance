# Project Governance Model

# Purpose

This document defines how every project is governed across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Project governance exists to ensure that projects remain aligned with enterprise strategy, governance principles, engineering standards and organizational objectives while preserving execution autonomy.

---

# Governance Principles

Every project shall be:

- strategically aligned
- human-governed
- evidence-based
- transparent
- auditable
- measurable
- documented
- continuously monitored

---

# Governance Structure

Every project shall include, at minimum:

- Executive Sponsor
- Project Owner
- Project Manager (or equivalent)
- Project Team
- Governance Oversight
- Stakeholders

Roles may be adapted according to project size.

---

# Governance Levels

Project governance operates across four levels.

Level 1

Strategic Governance

Responsible for:

- strategic alignment
- investment authorization
- executive oversight

Normally performed by executive leadership.

---

Level 2

Operational Governance

Responsible for:

- planning
- execution monitoring
- resource coordination
- delivery supervision

Normally performed by the project manager.

---

Level 3

Technical Governance

Responsible for:

- engineering quality
- architecture
- security
- documentation
- compliance

Normally performed by technical leaders.

---

Level 4

Execution Teams

Responsible for delivering approved work according to project plans.

Execution teams shall not redefine governance.

---

# Decision Authority

Strategic decisions remain exclusively human responsibilities.

Examples include:

- project approval
- budget approval
- scope changes
- cancellation
- suspension
- organizational priority

These decisions shall never be delegated to AI systems.

---

# Role of BEiA

BEiA acts exclusively as a Governance Intelligence System.

BEiA may provide:

- impact analysis
- dependency mapping
- schedule simulations
- governance compliance reports
- documentation analysis
- risk identification
- evidence aggregation
- historical comparisons

BEiA shall never:

- approve projects
- reject projects
- vote
- allocate budgets
- replace governance authorities

Final authority always belongs to humans.

---

# Escalation

Projects shall escalate governance matters whenever:

- strategic conflicts exist
- authority is unclear
- policies conflict
- major risks emerge
- cross-enterprise coordination becomes necessary

Escalation follows the Governance Escalation Model.

---

# Transparency

Every governance decision shall include:

- decision
- authority
- rationale
- supporting evidence
- timestamp
- documentation references

Undocumented governance decisions shall not be considered official.

---

# Project Autonomy

Projects possess operational autonomy.

Projects may determine:

- internal planning
- execution methodology
- meeting cadence
- task organization

Projects shall not violate:

- Constitution
- Governance Policies
- Enterprise Standards
- Engineering Standards

Autonomy exists inside governance boundaries.

---

# Governance Reviews

Governance reviews shall occur throughout the project lifecycle.

Typical review moments include:

- Planning Approval
- Architecture Review
- Security Review
- Delivery Review
- Deployment Review
- Closure Review

Additional reviews may be requested whenever necessary.

---

# Compliance

Every project operating within the BHG ecosystem shall comply with this governance model.

Exceptions require formal Governance Council approval.
