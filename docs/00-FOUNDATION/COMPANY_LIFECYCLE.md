# Company Lifecycle

## Purpose

This document defines the official lifecycle that governs every company belonging to the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

The objective is to ensure that companies evolve under predictable, auditable and scalable governance principles.

---

# Principles

Every company shall:

- exist for a defined strategic purpose;
- contribute measurable value to the ecosystem;
- operate under delegated authority;
- remain aligned with the BHG Constitution;
- evolve through documented governance.

No company exists independently from the ecosystem.

---

# Lifecycle

Every company passes through the following stages.

---

## Stage 1 — Concept

An opportunity is identified.

Activities include:

- problem definition
- market validation
- strategic alignment
- ecosystem impact analysis

Deliverable:

Business Concept Proposal.

---

## Stage 2 — Approval

The Governance Council evaluates whether the proposal aligns with the Holding strategy.

Evaluation includes:

- strategic fit
- legal feasibility
- financial sustainability
- governance impact
- technological compatibility

Deliverable:

Approval Resolution.

---

## Stage 3 — Foundation

The company becomes an official member of the ecosystem.

Mandatory activities include:

- company identity
- governance structure
- operating model
- documentation repository
- constitutional adoption

Deliverable:

Company Constitution.

---

## Stage 4 — Development

The organization begins building products, services and operational capabilities.

Activities include:

- engineering
- finance
- legal
- hiring
- partnerships
- AI integration

Deliverable:

Operational Readiness.

---

## Stage 5 — Growth

The company scales operations while preserving governance quality.

Activities include:

- market expansion
- process optimization
- compliance monitoring
- risk management
- organizational maturity

Deliverable:

Growth Review.

---

## Stage 6 — Maturity

The company becomes a stable strategic asset.

Characteristics include:

- predictable governance
- sustainable operations
- financial resilience
- organizational independence
- ecosystem contribution

Deliverable:

Maturity Assessment.

---

## Stage 7 — Transformation

When necessary, companies may evolve into:

- new business units
- subsidiaries
- regional organizations
- sub-holdings
- independent legal entities

Transformation shall preserve governance continuity.

Deliverable:

Transformation Plan.

---

## Stage 8 — Retirement

If strategic value no longer exists, a company may be retired.

Retirement shall preserve:

- legal obligations
- historical documentation
- intellectual property
- contractual commitments
- audit records

Deliverable:

Retirement Report.

---

# Governance Rules

Companies may not skip lifecycle stages unless formally approved by the Governance Council.

Each transition requires documented evidence.

---

# Human Authority

Lifecycle decisions belong exclusively to authorized human governance bodies.

Artificial Intelligence may assist by producing:

- analyses
- simulations
- recommendations
- documentation

Artificial Intelligence shall never approve lifecycle transitions.

---

# Audit Requirements

Every lifecycle event shall be:

- documented
- versioned
- reviewable
- traceable
- reproducible

No undocumented transition shall be considered valid.

---

# Compliance

Failure to follow this lifecycle constitutes a governance deviation and shall be formally reviewed.
