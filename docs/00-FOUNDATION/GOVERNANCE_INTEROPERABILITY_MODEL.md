# Governance Interoperability Model

> Defines how independent governance domains interact while preserving constitutional consistency across the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official interoperability model governing interactions between governance domains within the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Its objective is to ensure that autonomous entities can collaborate, exchange information and evolve independently without compromising constitutional integrity, governance consistency or institutional traceability.

---

# Interoperability Principle

Independent governance domains shall remain interoperable through shared governance principles rather than centralized operational control.

Interoperability shall preserve autonomy while ensuring institutional consistency.

---

# Governance Domains

Governance interoperability applies to:

- Holding Governance
- Subholding Governance
- Company Governance
- Project Governance
- Service Governance
- Engineering Governance
- AI Governance
- Automation Governance
- Repository Governance

Each domain remains autonomous within its assigned authority.

---

# Shared Governance Foundation

Every governance domain shall share:

- Constitutional principles
- Governance hierarchy
- Authority model
- Versioning model
- Traceability model
- Document metadata model

Shared foundations shall remain consistent throughout the ecosystem.

---

# Interoperability Requirements

Every governance interaction shall preserve:

- Traceability
- Version compatibility
- Authority integrity
- Document identity
- Dependency consistency
- Governance compliance

---

# Communication Principles

Governance domains shall communicate through documented interfaces.

Interactions shall remain:

- Explicit
- Versioned
- Auditable
- Deterministic
- Backward compatible whenever possible

---

# Dependency Management

Cross-domain dependencies shall:

- Be explicitly declared.
- Reference canonical documents.
- Avoid circular dependencies.
- Preserve repository independence.

Dependency relationships shall remain continuously verifiable.

---

# Governance Compatibility

Governance domains shall remain compatible when:

- Constitutional authority is preserved.
- Shared standards remain unchanged.
- Authority chains remain valid.
- Governance metadata remains consistent.

Compatibility shall be verified during governance reviews.

---

# Conflict Prevention

Interoperability shall minimize governance conflicts through:

- Explicit authority ownership.
- Stable interfaces.
- Shared terminology.
- Canonical document identifiers.
- Version compatibility rules.

Conflicts shall be resolved according to the Governance Conflict Resolution Model.

---

# Automation Support

Automation systems may verify interoperability by inspecting:

- Governance metadata.
- Authority chains.
- Dependency graphs.
- Document relationships.
- Version compatibility.
- Compliance status.

Automation shall support governance without replacing human authority.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Autonomous governance creates resilient organizations.

> Interoperability transforms independent governance domains into one institutional ecosystem.
