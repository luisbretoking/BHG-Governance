# AI Agent Certification Standard

> Official Certification Framework for Artificial Intelligence Agents operating within Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

This standard establishes the official certification framework used to evaluate, classify, authorize and continuously validate AI agents operating within the BHG ecosystem.

Certification ensures that every AI agent meets the organizational, governance, security and operational requirements defined by the Holding.

Only certified AI agents may operate on official repositories and governance artifacts.

---

# Certification Principles

Certification shall follow these principles:

- Governance First
- Human Authority
- Transparency
- Traceability
- Explainability
- Vendor Independence
- Continuous Verification
- Least Privilege

Certification is granted to operational behavior rather than to a specific AI model.

---

# Certification Objectives

Certification ensures that an AI agent:

- behaves consistently
- follows governance
- protects institutional knowledge
- preserves repository integrity
- produces auditable outputs
- respects organizational authority

---

# Certification Levels

## Level 1 — Foundation

The AI agent may:

- read documentation
- search repositories
- answer questions
- generate summaries

No modification capability is allowed.

---

## Level 2 — Professional

The AI agent may additionally:

- analyze documentation
- generate implementation proposals
- perform impact analysis
- validate references
- review metadata

Human approval is always required.

---

## Level 3 — Enterprise

The AI agent may additionally:

- prepare documentation updates
- prepare engineering artifacts
- execute approved documentation workflows
- assist organizational processes

All implementations remain subject to governance.

---

## Level 4 — Governance

The AI agent may additionally:

- audit repositories
- validate governance compliance
- identify constitutional conflicts
- evaluate organizational consistency
- prepare governance reports

Governance authority remains exclusively human.

---

## Level 5 — Autonomous Operations

The AI agent may execute pre-approved repetitive operations such as:

- documentation formatting
- metadata normalization
- link verification
- repository validation
- automated compliance checks

Governance modifications are permanently excluded.

---

# Certification Requirements

Every certification shall verify:

- capability profile
- governance compliance
- operational behavior
- auditability
- traceability
- reproducibility
- security compliance
- documentation quality

---

# Certification Lifecycle

Every certified AI agent shall follow the lifecycle below.

```
Registration

↓

Capability Assessment

↓

Governance Validation

↓

Operational Evaluation

↓

Certification Review

↓

Human Approval

↓

Certification Issued

↓

Continuous Monitoring

↓

Periodic Reassessment

↓

Renewal or Revocation
```

---

# Continuous Compliance

Certification is not permanent.

Certified AI agents shall undergo periodic reassessment.

Loss of compliance may result in:

- suspension
- restricted permissions
- certification downgrade
- certification revocation

---

# Certification Authority

Only the BHG Governance Council may issue, suspend or revoke AI certifications.

AI agents may assist the certification process but shall never certify themselves or other agents.

---

# Certification Record

Each certified AI agent shall maintain an official certification record including:

- unique identifier
- certification level
- approved capabilities
- approval date
- review history
- operational status
- certification authority

---

# Governance as Code

Certification requirements shall be machine-verifiable whenever technically feasible.

Automated validation shall support, but never replace, human certification decisions.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Capability enables action.

> Certification enables trust.

> Governance authorizes operation.

> Human authority grants legitimacy.
