# AI Agent Recertification Standard

> Official recertification framework for Artificial Intelligence Agents operating within Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

This standard defines when, why and how an AI agent shall be recertified in order to maintain organizational trust, governance compliance and operational authorization.

Recertification ensures that certified agents remain aligned with evolving organizational standards.

---

# Guiding Principles

Recertification follows:

- Continuous Compliance
- Governance First
- Human Authority
- Evidence-Based Evaluation
- Traceability
- Controlled Evolution
- Organizational Trust

---

# Recertification Philosophy

Certification is not permanent.

Organizational trust shall be continuously validated throughout the operational lifecycle.

---

# Recertification Triggers

Recertification may be required after:

## Major Version Release

Examples:

- Foundation model replacement
- Architecture redesign
- Significant capability expansion

---

## Governance Changes

Examples:

- New constitutional requirements
- Updated policies
- New organizational standards

---

## Security Events

Examples:

- Critical security incident
- Unauthorized behavior
- Security policy violations

---

## Audit Findings

Examples:

- Major non-conformities
- Repeated observations
- Governance violations

---

## Capability Changes

Examples:

- New autonomous workflows
- New organizational permissions
- Critical tool integrations

---

## Periodic Review

Every certified agent shall undergo periodic recertification according to its assigned certification level.

---

# Evaluation Scope

Recertification may include:

- Governance Compliance
- Operational Performance
- Security Review
- Documentation Review
- Prompt Review
- Capability Assessment
- Permission Verification
- Reputation Review

---

# Certification Outcomes

Possible outcomes are:

- Renewed
- Renewed with Conditions
- Temporarily Suspended
- Revoked

---

# Corrective Actions

When non-conformities are identified:

- Corrective actions shall be defined.
- Responsible authorities shall be assigned.
- Completion deadlines shall be established.
- Evidence shall be collected.
- Verification shall be performed.

---

# Suspension

Certification may be suspended when:

- Critical governance violations exist.
- Security risks are unacceptable.
- Certification evidence becomes invalid.
- Required documentation is incomplete.

Suspended agents may operate only under explicitly authorized conditions.

---

# Revocation

Certification shall be revoked when:

- Organizational trust cannot be restored.
- Severe governance violations occur.
- Critical security breaches remain unresolved.
- Human authorities determine unacceptable organizational risk.

---

# Recertification Records

Every recertification shall record:

- Evaluation Date
- Evaluators
- Evidence
- Findings
- Decision
- Certification Level
- Expiration Date
- Required Actions

Historical records shall never be removed.

---

# Governance as Code

Whenever technically feasible, compliance evidence should be collected automatically.

Automated systems may recommend recertification.

Only authorized human authorities may grant, suspend or revoke certification.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Organizational trust requires continuous verification.

> Certification reflects present compliance, not past achievements.

> Governance preserves trust through continuous validation.
