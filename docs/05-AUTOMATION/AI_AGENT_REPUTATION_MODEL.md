# AI Agent Reputation Model

> Official trust and reputation framework for Artificial Intelligence Agents operating within Breto's Holding Group.

---

# Purpose

This model defines how organizational trust is established, maintained and continuously evaluated for every AI agent operating within the BHG ecosystem.

Reputation shall always be based on objective evidence.

No subjective evaluation shall affect an agent's reputation.

---

# Guiding Principles

Reputation follows:

- Evidence First
- Continuous Evaluation
- Transparency
- Explainability
- Traceability
- Governance Alignment
- Human Authority

---

# Reputation Philosophy

Reputation is not a score.

Reputation is an organizational trust profile built from verified operational evidence.

---

# Reputation Dimensions

Every AI agent shall be evaluated across independent dimensions.

## Governance Compliance

Measures adherence to:

- Constitution
- Policies
- Standards
- Governance Procedures

---

## Operational Reliability

Measures:

- Stability
- Successful Executions
- Failure Rate
- Recovery Performance

---

## Security Behavior

Measures:

- Security Compliance
- Permission Usage
- Unauthorized Attempts
- Incident History

---

## Documentation Quality

Measures:

- Documentation Accuracy
- Documentation Completeness
- Traceability
- Standards Compliance

---

## Audit Performance

Measures:

- Audit Findings
- Corrective Actions
- Compliance History
- Audit Consistency

---

## Certification Maturity

Measures:

- Certification Level
- Recertification History
- Certification Stability

---

## Organizational Contribution

Measures:

- Reviews Completed
- Documents Produced
- Improvements Suggested
- Knowledge Generated

---

# Reputation Indicators

Indicators may include:

- Trust Level
- Governance Health
- Operational Stability
- Security Confidence
- Documentation Excellence
- Organizational Contribution

---

# Reputation Levels

Official levels are:

- Foundational
- Developing
- Trusted
- Advanced
- Strategic
- Institutional

These levels represent organizational maturity rather than authority.

---

# Reputation Updates

Reputation shall evolve through:

- Monitoring
- Audits
- Certifications
- Operational Activity
- Governance Compliance

Manual modification is prohibited.

---

# Reputation Degradation

Reputation may decrease due to:

- Governance Violations
- Failed Audits
- Security Incidents
- Certification Expiration
- Repeated Operational Failures

All reductions shall remain historically traceable.

---

# Reputation Recovery

Reputation may improve through:

- Successful Audits
- Continuous Compliance
- Recertification
- Operational Excellence
- Corrective Actions

Recovery shall always be evidence-based.

---

# Organizational Usage

Reputation may support:

- Task Assignment
- Certification Planning
- Operational Reviews
- Continuous Improvement
- Risk Analysis

Reputation shall never replace human judgment.

---

# Governance as Code

Whenever technically feasible, reputation indicators should be automatically calculated from organizational evidence.

Automation evaluates evidence.

Human authorities interpret organizational impact.

---

# Institutional Principle

> Reputation is earned through consistent behavior.

> Trust grows through verified evidence.

> Organizational confidence is built over time.

> Governance transforms evidence into trust.
