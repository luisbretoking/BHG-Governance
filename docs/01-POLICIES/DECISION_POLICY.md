# Decision Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the mandatory framework for decision-making within the Breto's Holding Group ecosystem.

Its purpose is to ensure that strategic, tactical and operational decisions are:

- made by authorized governance roles;
- supported by evidence;
- properly documented;
- traceable throughout their lifecycle;
- aligned with institutional principles.

Decision-making is considered a core governance capability of BHG.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- all governance bodies;
- all companies within the BHG ecosystem;
- all products and services;
- all projects;
- all repositories;
- all institutional decisions generating measurable impact.

Any decision affecting governance, operations, architecture, compliance, strategy or institutional assets shall follow this policy.

---

# Normative Authority

## Governed By

This policy derives authority from the BHG Constitution and the governance framework established by the Fundamental Governance Models.

## Governs

This policy establishes the mandatory rules applicable to institutional decision processes throughout the ecosystem.

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1 — Evidence-Based Decisions

Every relevant decision shall be supported by sufficient evidence, analysis and contextual information.

Personal preference or unsupported assumptions shall not constitute adequate decision justification.

---

## Principle 2 — Competent Authority

Only governance roles with formally delegated authority may approve or execute decisions within their defined scope.

Authority shall always be derived from the Authority Model and Authority Matrix.

## Principle 3 — Decision Traceability

Every institutional decision shall preserve sufficient information to reconstruct:

- decision origin;
- responsible authority;
- decision context;
- evaluated alternatives;
- supporting evidence;
- expected impact;
- implementation result;
- related governance artifacts.

Decision traceability is mandatory for institutional continuity and auditability.

---

## Principle 4 — Proportional Governance

The level of documentation, review and approval required for a decision shall be proportional to its potential impact.

Higher-impact decisions require:

- greater evidence requirements;
- broader impact analysis;
- higher approval authority;
- stronger audit requirements.

---

## Principle 5 — Transparency

Decisions shall be accessible to authorized participants together with their justification, evidence and governance context.

Confidential information shall remain protected according to the applicable classification rules.

---

## Principle 6 — Continuous Review

Decisions may be reviewed when:

- new evidence becomes available;
- strategic conditions change;
- governance requirements evolve;
- implementation results differ from expectations.

A revised decision shall never erase the historical record of the original decision.

---

# Decision Classification

Every institutional decision shall be classified according to its impact level.

---

## Strategic Decisions

Strategic decisions affect the long-term direction, structure or identity of the Holding ecosystem.

Examples:

- corporate direction;
- acquisitions;
- creation of new strategic domains;
- governance changes;
- major architectural decisions.

Strategic decisions require the highest level of governance review and approval.

---

## Tactical Decisions

Tactical decisions affect specific programs, departments, domains, companies or projects.

Examples:

- resource allocation;
- project prioritization;
- operational model changes;
- domain-level initiatives.

Tactical decisions shall be approved by the competent authority defined in the Authority Matrix.

---

## Operational Decisions

Operational decisions affect daily execution activities and local processes.

Examples:

- task execution;
- process adjustments;
- routine operational choices;
- implementation details.

Operational decisions shall remain aligned with all applicable higher-level governance artifacts.

---

# Decision Lifecycle

Every institutional decision shall follow the following lifecycle:

```
Problem or Opportunity Identification

↓

Evidence Collection

↓

Alternative Analysis

↓

Risk Assessment

↓

Decision Evaluation

↓

Approval Decision

↓

Formal Registration

↓

Implementation

↓

Monitoring

↓

Review (when applicable)

↓

Historical Archive
```

No strategic decision may bypass formal registration.

---

# Decision Registration Requirements

Every institutional decision record shall include, at minimum:

- decision identifier;
- decision title;
- decision classification;
- responsible authority;
- decision date;
- context description;
- problem or opportunity identified;
- alternatives evaluated;
- selected option;
- supporting evidence;
- expected impact;
- implementation owner;
- related documents;
- review date when applicable.

---

# Decision Evolution

Decisions shall evolve through new decisions.

A previous decision shall never be overwritten or deleted.

Every new decision modifying an existing decision shall preserve:

- previous decision reference;
- reason for change;
- new evidence;
- updated approval authority;
- impact assessment.

- # BKOs Compatibility

BKOs shall support institutional decision governance by:

- locating decision records;
- connecting decisions with related projects, documents and assets;
- identifying dependencies;
- reconstructing complete decision history;
- preserving decision context;
- supporting governance audits.

BKOs may provide recommendations, context analysis and historical insights.

BKOs shall never approve, reject or modify institutional decisions.

Final decision authority remains exclusively with authorized human governance bodies.

---

# BEiA and Artificial Intelligence Compatibility

Authorized AI systems, including BEiA, may assist decision processes by:

- analyzing available evidence;
- identifying inconsistencies;
- comparing alternatives;
- simulating potential impacts;
- generating decision summaries;
- identifying related governance artifacts;
- recommending possible improvements.

AI-generated analysis is advisory only.

AI systems shall never:

- make binding institutional decisions;
- approve governance decisions;
- replace human authority;
- bypass the Authority Model.

---

# Governance as Code

Decision governance shall be structured to support automated verification.

Governance systems may:

- validate decision records;
- verify authority permissions;
- detect missing evidence;
- confirm required approvals;
- analyze decision dependencies;
- monitor decision lifecycle compliance;
- generate governance reports.

Automation supports decision governance.

Human authority governs decisions.

---

# Audit

Every institutional decision shall remain auditable.

Audits may evaluate:

- compliance with this policy;
- authority legitimacy;
- evidence quality;
- decision rationale;
- implementation results;
- governance alignment;
- historical traceability.

A decision without sufficient traceability shall be considered a governance non-conformity.

---

# Compliance

All institutional decisions within the BHG ecosystem shall comply with this policy.

The following shall be considered governance non-conformities:

- decisions made without competent authority;
- strategic decisions without formal registration;
- decisions without supporting evidence;
- missing decision history;
- unauthorized modification of decision records;
- bypassing approved governance processes.

Non-conformities shall be managed according to the Governance Conflict Resolution process.

---

# Final Principle

Decisions define the evolution of Breto's Holding Group.

A strong governance system does not eliminate uncertainty.

It ensures that uncertainty is managed through evidence, authority, documentation and institutional learning.

Every relevant decision shall contribute to the continuity, coherence and long-term development of the BHG ecosystem.
